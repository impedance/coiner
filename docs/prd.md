# Product Requirements Document: Moneywork

## 1. Vision and Mission

**Moneywork** is a lightweight financial behavior tracker designed for iPhone. It is built to bridge the gap between financial intention and daily repetition.

### Core Philosophy: "80% Psychology / 20% Mechanics"
The app is based on the "Master of Money" course principles. While traditional finance tools focus on deep accounting and automation, Moneywork focuses on:
- **Awareness**: Capturing every transaction manually to stay "awake" to money movement.
- **Intentionality**: Giving every dollar a job (The Bucket System).
- **Safety & Joy**: Prioritizing the Reserve (safety) and the Joy Fund (motivation) as first-class citizens.
- **Integration**: Closing the loop through Weekly Reviews and Practice Cycles to turn actions into permanent habits.

---

## 2. Product Principles

- **Capture First**: If daily capture fails, the whole system fails. The UI must optimize for a <10s entry flow.
- **Behavior over Accounting**: We prioritize "did you do the practice?" over "is your balance accurate to the cent?".
- **Local-First & Private**: Financial data is sensitive. It stays on the phone. No clouds, no accounts, no tracking.
- **No-Shame UX**: Mistakes and missed days happen. The app should encourage getting back on track rather than punishing failure.
- **The Mandatory Ritual**: The Weekly Review is not a "report" you might read; it is a required step to prepare for the next week.

---

## 3. The Behavioral OS (Core Loops)

### Daily Loop: The Awareness Practice
1. **Capture**: Record income/expense immediately.
2. **Assign**: Ensure no money stays "Unassigned" for long.
3. **Check-in**: Mark progress on the current Practice Cycle.

### Weekly Loop: The Reflection Ritual
1. **Fact Check**: See what actually happened vs. the plan.
2. **Identify Leaks**: Spot overspent categories without judgment.
3. **Adjust**: Update buckets for the coming week.
4. **Focus**: Set one primary behavior intent for next week.

### Monthly Loop: The Strategy
1. **Plan**: Set the high-level bucket targets.
2. **Step-Up**: Evaluate if it's time to move to the next "Money Step" (norm upgrade).

---

## 4. Key Behavioral Mechanics

### The Bucket System (Planning)
Inspired by YNAB but simplified. Money is allocated to:
- **Primary**: Necessary expenses.
- **Reserve**: The safety buffer (Emergency Fund).
- **Joy Fund**: Money specifically designated for guilt-free pleasure to prevent burnout.
- **Goals**: Long-term targets with emotional anchors.

### Practice Cycles (14/21/30 Days)
Behavior changes through repetition. Users choose "Cycles" to reinforce specific habits:
- **Capture All**: Training the muscle of recording everything.
- **Reserve First**: Prioritizing the safety buffer on every income.
- **Joy Awareness**: Ensuring the internal "child" is rewarded.

### Money Steps (Level of Norm)
Progress isn't just about more money; it's about a higher "level of norm."
- **Minimum**: Basic stability and tracking.
- **Optimum**: Regular savings and comfortable lifestyle.
- **Maximum**: Freedom and advanced lifestyle upgrades.

### Emotional Anchors (Artifacts)
Goals are not just numbers; they are "Artifacts." A goal can have a photo or a description of *why* it matters, acting as a visual reminder of the "Future Self" the user is building.

## 5. Glossary of Terms

| Term | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Capture** | The act of manually recording a transaction. |
| **Bucket** | A category with a specific "job" assigned to its balance. |
| **Unassigned** | Money that has been recorded as income but not yet given a job. |
| **Reserve** | A system bucket for security and emergency savings. |
| **Joy Fund** | A system bucket for guilt-free spending on personal happiness. |
| **Artifact** | A visual or text-based emotional reminder attached to a Goal. |
| **Cycle** | A time-bound period (14-30 days) focused on habit reinforcement. |
| **Practice** | A specific daily or weekly action (e.g., "Capture All"). |
| **Weekly Review** | The ritual of reflecting on the past week and planning the next. |
| **Money Step** | A milestone representing an upgrade in the user's "standard of living." |

---

## 6. Target User (Job to be Done)

**The Solo Optimizer**: 
*"Help me stay aware of my money every day, assign it intentionally, keep building safety and joy, and reinforce the financial behaviors I actually want to live by without the overhead of complex accounting software."*
